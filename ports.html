Overview of Colombo Port

Port of Colombo, located on Sri Lanka ' southwestern coast, is one of South Asia ' busiest ports and a key transshipment hub for East-West maritime trade. With terminals like the Jaya Container Terminal and South Asia Gateway Terminal and CICT, it boasts a high capacity of TEUs. Port of Colombo with variety of berths, is the major port in Sri Lanka that has facilities to accommodate all type of vessels including but not limited to break bulk, tankers, Naval and cruise vessels. Recent expansions aim to enhance capacity and promote sustainability, while its strategic location offers quick turnaround times and comprehensive support services for all shipping agents.

Galle Harbour Overview

Galle Harbour, a natural harbor situated on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka, is among the most dynamic regional ports in the country. Furthermore, Galle uniquely stands out as the only port in Sri Lanka offering specialized facilities for pleasure yachts. Recognized by international yacht societies, Galle Harbour is celebrated as one of the premier global destinations for yachting enthusiasts.

Port of Trincomalee

Trincomalee Harbour, renowned as the second-best natural harbor in the world, boasts a water and land area approximately ten times larger than that of the Port of Colombo. This strategic location has been earmarked for handling bulk and breakbulk cargo.
